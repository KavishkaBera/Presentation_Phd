Justifying the 500-Nest Sample

1. Machine Learning Split

Neural networks require sufficient data to prevent overfitting. A 80/20 split yields 400 training nests and 100 validation nests, providing a statistically significant baseline to evaluate our 85% accuracy goal.

2. Statistical Power (EPV)

Predictive modeling requires 20-50 "Events Per Variable" (EPV) for stability. 9 variables × 50 events = 450. Targeting 500 gives an excellent 50-nest safety margin for field anomalies.

3. Spatio-Temporal Grid

Across 5 rookeries and 2 monsoon seasons, 500 nests divide into ~50 nests per spatio-temporal block. This is the minimum threshold required to perform rigorous spatial block cross-validation.

4. Ecological Feasibility

For an endangered species, 10,000 nests is practically impossible for a single researcher. 500 is the "Sweet Spot"—ethically observable, accurately measurable, and safely achievable within 24 months.
